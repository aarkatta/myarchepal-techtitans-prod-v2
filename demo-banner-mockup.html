Anatomy & rationale

Why amber, not red or blue?

Amber matches your existing "pending" / "Template Reviews" accent. Red implies error; blue implies neutral info. Amber says "heads up — there's an opportunity here."

Why a top banner, not a toast?

Toasts dismiss themselves. This message needs to stay visible across pages until the user acts. A persistent top banner is the right pattern for environment-level context.

Why dismissible per session?

Repeat users who've seen it 20 times will appreciate hiding it during a working session, but it returns on next login so the path to a private org stays visible.

Why "Contact us" not the raw email?

Verb-led link copy is more inviting and accessible. The mailto link can pre-fill a subject line so inbound leads are tagged automatically.
